Strategy roadmap

Move from fundamentals to advanced decision making.

Preflop foundations

Build disciplined opening, calling, 3-betting, and squeezing ranges. Start tighter in early position and widen steadily as you gain positional advantage.

  • Prioritize big pairs, big aces, and suited Broadway hands first.
  • Use suited connectors and wheel aces more often when stacks are deeper.
  • Avoid dominated offsuit hands out of position.

Position awareness

Position is one of the largest edges in poker. You realize equity better, control pot size, and gather more information when acting later.

  • Open fewer hands UTG and more hands on the button.
  • Defend blinds selectively and plan postflop lines before you call.
  • Attack capped ranges when you have last action.

Postflop play

Use range advantage, nut advantage, and board texture to decide when to c-bet, check back, value bet thinly, or bluff with the right blockers.

  • Bet more often on dry ace-high and king-high boards.
  • Slow down on coordinated boards that hit the caller harder.
  • Choose draw bluffs that retain equity when called.

Poker math shortcuts

Great players convert probabilities into fast in-game decisions. Pot odds, implied odds, stack-to-pot ratio, and MDF are practical tools—not just theory.

  • Compare call price to your estimated equity.
  • Use the rule of 2 and 4 for quick draw approximations.
  • Respect SPR: one-pair hands shrink in value as SPR rises.

Tournament adjustments

Antes, payout pressure, and shallower stacks change optimal ranges. Learn when to raise-fold, reshove, or pressure medium stacks.

  • Widen late-position opens at 20–40bb.
  • Adopt push/fold logic as stacks dip below 15bb.
  • Preserve ICM awareness near bubbles and pay jumps.

Exploitative strategy

Baseline theory keeps you balanced, but population reads create profit. Adjust when opponents under-bluff, over-fold, limp too much, or call too wide.

  • Value bet bigger against calling stations.
  • Bluff less when players hate folding river.
  • Isolation raise limpers who enter too many pots.
